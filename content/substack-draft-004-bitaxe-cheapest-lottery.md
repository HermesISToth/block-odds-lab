There is a reason the BitAxe keeps pulling people in.

It is not because a desk-size miner is likely to find a Bitcoin block. It is not because a 1 TH/s to 2 TH/s device should be treated like an income machine. It is because the BitAxe turns Bitcoin mining from an abstract industrial-scale thing into something you can actually see, hear, tune, measure, and understand on a desk.

That makes it one of the most honest lottery tickets in Bitcoin.

The current small-miner lineup is easy to understand:

- BitAxe Ultra: a lower-cost BM1366 route, often around the few-hundred-GH/s range.
- BitAxe Supra: a more efficient BM1368 route.
- BitAxe Gamma: the popular BM1370 route, often discussed around 1.2 TH/s.
- Higher-pushed Gamma/GT-style setups: more hashrate, more heat, more reason to watch stability.
- Multi-chip boards: more serious, more power, less desk-toy simplicity.

The Gamma is the one I keep coming back to because it sits in a sweet spot. It feels like a real miner, but it is still small enough that the pitch should stay honest: learning, tuning, and lottery-mining fun.

The important part comes after the hook.

At roughly terahash scale, the odds are real but tiny. You are competing against the whole Bitcoin network. You can technically find a block, but the expected time is the whole point. That is why I do not like presenting these miners as predictable earners. The cleaner pitch is:

1. Buy it if you want to learn.
2. Run it if you enjoy the lottery.
3. Tune it if you like the hardware.
4. Measure it before pretending another board changes everything.

I made a Gamma-specific odds route here:

[Gamma odds route: https://blockoddslabs.com/bitaxe-gamma-odds.html](https://blockoddslabs.com/bitaxe-gamma-odds.html)

Use it before chasing a hotter setting or buying a second board. The questions that matter are simple:

- What hashrate does the miner actually hold after hours, not seconds?
- What does it pull at the wall?
- Are rejects or heat eating the value of the pushed tune?
- Does a second board move the yearly odds enough to matter?
- Would a better fan, PSU, meter, or cooler setup be a smarter first upgrade?

There is also a deeper reason people like these little miners: they make Bitcoin mining feel less centralized and less invisible. A small BitAxe on a desk is not going to outcompete an industrial farm, but it does let a regular person participate directly in the network. That matters culturally, even when the odds are long.

So yes, it is a lottery ticket. But unlike a normal lottery ticket, it teaches you about difficulty, hashrate, power, thermals, pools, variance, and why Bitcoin mining is so brutally competitive.

That is the honest version of the pitch.

Watch the Gamma, run the odds, and buy it for the right reason.
