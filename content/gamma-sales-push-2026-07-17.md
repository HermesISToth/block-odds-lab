Title: The Bitaxe Gamma Video Did Not Need Big Views To Sell

A small but useful signal came out of the latest Bitaxe Gamma push: the video did not need massive reach to produce sales. That matters because the Gamma is not a broad impulse gadget. It is a product for a specific buyer: someone curious enough about Bitcoin mining to want a real desk-size ASIC miner, but not so deep that they are already building a full farm.

That is exactly where honest math helps.

The Gamma is a great little learning machine. It lets you see real hashrate, watts, heat, pool behavior, rejects, tuning tradeoffs, and the emotional pull of lottery mining. But it should not be sold as predictable income. The better pitch is simple:

1. Watch the hardware.
2. Understand what it can do.
3. Run the odds before buying another board or pushing the settings.
