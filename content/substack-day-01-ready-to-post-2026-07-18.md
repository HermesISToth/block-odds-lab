## Reply 1 - Bitaxe / Small ASIC Thread

The "second board" question is where the math gets useful. If one small ASIC is fun, two can be fun too, but the odds change by hashrate, not vibes.

For a Gamma I would compare stable hashrate, wall watts, current difficulty, and yearly solo chance before buying another board.

Gamma route: https://blockoddslabs.com/bitaxe-gamma-odds.html

## Reply 2 - Solo Mining Odds Thread

Expected time is easy to misread as a countdown. It is not. Every hash is a fresh probability event against current difficulty.

That is why I like running the calculator before talking about "possible" versus "probable."

Calculator: https://blockoddslabs.com/block-odds-calculator.html

## Reply 3 - Hardware / Tuning Thread

For small miners, the underrated upgrade is often stability: cleaner power, better cooling, fewer rejects, and measured watts before pushing frequency.

The screenshot hashrate matters less if the setup cannot hold it overnight.

Gamma tuning: https://blockoddslabs.com/articles/bitaxe-gamma-tuning.html

## Restack Comment
