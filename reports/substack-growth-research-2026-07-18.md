## Core Takeaway

BOL's problem is not just post quality. It is lack of initial distribution. Substack posts do not automatically receive meaningful discovery when the publication has one subscriber and little network activity.

The best near-term growth path is:

1. Publish posts that are useful for months, not just same-day announcements.
2. Turn each post into several Notes with a clear value proposition.
3. Spend more time replying/restacking in the correct Substack scene than publishing into an empty room.
4. Build recommendation relationships with adjacent Bitcoin, creator-tech, personal finance, and hobbyist hardware publications.
5. Use TikTok Gamma, BOL site, and Bitcointalk as external traffic sparks that send qualified readers back to the Substack and BOL calculators.

## Why BOL Has Low Views

- One subscriber means email distribution is basically zero.
- New posts need a network signal: Notes engagement, restacks, recommendations, existing audience, outside traffic, or search.
- Posting only long-form articles is too passive for a new publication.
- BOL's niche is useful but narrow, so Notes should target exact reader moments: Bitaxe curiosity, NerdMiner disappointment, solo-mining odds confusion, tuning/power questions, and second-board buying decisions.

## BOL 14-Day Substack Visibility Loop

Daily:

- Post 1 useful Note from the BOL account.
- Leave 3 thoughtful replies on relevant Substack Notes or posts.
- Restack 1 relevant creator or publication with a real comment, not a generic boost.
- Check Note stats and subscriber/follower source data after 24 hours.

Twice per week:

- Publish or refresh one evergreen post.
- Restack one older BOL article with a new hook.
- Cross-link the post from BOL's Notes hub and most relevant calculator page.

Weekly:

- Add or refine 3-5 recommendations for adjacent publications with real blurbs.
- Identify 5 candidate creators/publications for genuine engagement before asking for anything.
- Review Stats: Traffic, Growth, Notes, Network, follower count, free subscribers, and link clicks.
