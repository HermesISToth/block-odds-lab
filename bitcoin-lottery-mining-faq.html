Can a Bitaxe find a Bitcoin block?

Yes. A Bitaxe can technically find a Bitcoin block, and real small-miner jackpot stories are part of why people pay attention. The honest answer does not stop there. At hobby-miner hashrates, the probability is still tiny, so the useful question is what your stable hashrate, wall watts, current difficulty, and time horizon imply.

Use the Bitaxe odds calculator for general Bitaxe math or the Bitaxe Gamma odds page when the conversation is specifically about a Gamma.

Can a NerdMiner find a Bitcoin block?

A NerdMiner can technically find a block, but possible is not the same as probable. For most people, its stronger value is learning: seeing shares, pool stats, addresses, and mining concepts in a low-cost way.

Send beginner threads to the NerdMiner Bitcoin odds route before turning the conversation into ROI or jackpot talk.

Is Bitcoin lottery mining profitable?

Usually not in the predictable-income sense that buyers want. A lottery miner can be fun, educational, and occasionally newsworthy, but expected value depends on current difficulty, Bitcoin price, block reward, hashrate, fees, power cost, and hardware cost.

For profit claims, use the lottery-mining profitability reality check and then rerun the calculator with current inputs.

Does buying a second small miner help?

It helps only in the narrow sense that more hashrate increases the chance. It does not make the setup predictable income. If the first miner is unstable, too hot, badly powered, or measured only by software-reported watts, the better upgrade may be cooling, a meter, or power stability.

Use the starter kit path before buying another board, then compare one-board versus two-board math in the calculator.

Why did my old odds screenshot change?

Bitcoin difficulty changes roughly every 2,016 blocks. When difficulty moves, the same miner has different expected time and yearly odds than it had before. Old screenshots can be useful examples, but they should not be treated as current math.

Use the Bitcoin mining difficulty tracker when a thread is about retargets, stale odds, or why the calculator result changed.
