Solo mining

Choosing a solo pool is mostly about execution

A solo pool does not make your miner luckier. It gives your miner a place to submit work and a way to claim a block if luck hits.

Check these before settling in

  • Clear payout rules and fees.
  • Stable connection from your location.
  • Visible worker stats and accepted shares.
  • Reasonable response time.
  • Fallback pool support on your miner.
  • Transparent block and payout history.

What does not change

Your odds still come from your hashrate divided by network difficulty. Pool choice protects execution; it does not rewrite probability.

Mining outcomes are random. Nothing here is financial advice.
